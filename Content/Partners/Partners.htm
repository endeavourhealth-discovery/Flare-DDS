Our mission at LHS is to demonstrate, by making multiple sources of data available to multiple end users, that interoperability at a reasonable cost, is available to the NHS. LHS, partnered with the London Discovery Programme and Endeavour Heath Charitable Trust, design, develop, implement, host, and support the NHS Discovery Data Service.
We have worked closely with NHS colleague to develop a learning health system that will help the NHS improve clinical quality, reduce healthcare costs, and enable value propositions by providing a multi-source data platform.
Restart is the UK’s leading provider of end to end integration solutions for healthcare. 100% requirements-led, no one else gets your interoperability vision up and running faster. From point to point integration to complex system architectures, Restart get your data where it needs to be, in the right format, every time. Restart is working with Barking, Havering and Redbridge University Hospitals NHS Trust (BHR) to extract multiple data types from the data warehouse and deliver them to DDS, converting all extracts into the required DDS format. The partnership ensures subscribers have access to quality data that is standardised to facilitate analytics, decision making and planning.
Ripple Foundation is a clinically led team working towards and supporting the move to an open platform fit for 21st century health and care. Supporting open source, open standards and an open architecture.
The collaboration between the Ripple and Discovery teams enables the Helm Patient Portal to access GP data from practices using Vision, TPP, EMIS from a single source.
Factor 50 is an organisation focused on bringing together world class skills in the disciplines of Data, Analysis and Digital to improve health outcomes in the UK. In particular, Factor 50 is pleased to be partnering with LHS on the London Discovery Programme. We believe Discovery is at the cutting edge of using health data from multiple data sources and we are excited to be delivering some of the first demonstrator projects.

Dynamic Health Systems Ltd. was formed in January 2011 to design, build, and launch a service that supports the millions of people who live with long-term conditions (chronic illnesses) or who need complex care in a way to significantly improve health outcomes, improve patient experience, as well as reduce service utilisation. Our goal is to improve health outcomes to give patients an opportunity for a better life, improve service utilisation to give the health systems the potential to deliver better outcomes and, at the same time, make them financially sustainable. We chose to create a digital service to achieve this and named it 'VitruCare'. It is a Patient Engagement Platform that uses evidence based psychology to engage and motivate patients into co-creating healthcare, supports HCPs in co-ordinating service provision, and improves productivity and outcomes all round. Clinical studies demonstrate health outcome improvements.
