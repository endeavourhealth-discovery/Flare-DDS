East London Genes and Health

Project summary

The East London Genes and Health (ELG&H) study aims to improve the health of people of Pakistani and Bangladeshi heritage by analysing the genes and health of 100,000 local people.

The DDS genes and health extract provides relevant genomic and phenomic research information from patient GP electronic health record (with the consent of the GP practice data controllers in 4 CCGs - Newham, City and Hackney, Tower Hamlets and Waltham Forest).

The research aims are to study:

  • how genes normally vary from person to person in the adult Bangladeshi and Pakistani communities. Knowing what is normal is very important when searching for genes causing certain childhood diseases.
  • genes in people with very high and very low cholesterol levels, to better understand why heart disease and stroke occur.
  • variation in genes in healthy adults whose parents are related. These studies will tell us how genes work (especially knockouts), and help develop new medicines and treatments.
  • genes of people with diabetes, aiming to identify rarer types of diabetes for which specific treatments might be used.
  • how some people respond differently to certain medicines.

Project population

GP records available within the following CCGs: Tower Hamlets, Newham, Waltham Forest and City & Hackney.

Project cohort

People of Pakistani and Bangladeshi heritage.
